Preliminary Research

Before we met our users and started deep research we decided to explore the domain field. For this we conducted a preliminary research which comprised of: literature review and investigated online resources about pharmaceutical sales representatives, and competitive analysis on the CRM tools used in industry

Human-Centered Research Methods

We conducted our main research with both primary users, CSs, and Genentech home office representatives by applying various user-centered methods:

Contextual Inquiry

Contextual inquiry is human-centered research practice that involves observing people in their place of work carrying out their responsibilities. The researcher asks probing questions while the participant is doing their normal activities to understand how they do their work. At the end of a contextual interview, the researcher shares their interpretation of the participant's behavior and thoughts and allows the participant to correct them. We conducted three contextual inquiries with CS in a major metropolitan area. To fully immerse ourselves in their world of work, we spent 5-6 hours with each CS observing how they plan their lunch appointments and sales calls.

Shadowing

When a researcher silently follows a participant and notes down observations about their activities. The goal is to get a first-hand perspective of the participant's work practices and external actions. Since the CS's major responsibility is speaking to doctors interrupting their conversation would be inappropriate. It would not be possible to conduct a true contextual interview in this setting. We went on "ride-alongs" with CS in the field and saw firsthand their interactions with prescribers and medical staff, noting down our objective impressions of their communication.

Retrospective Interview (RI)

Retrospective Interviews were conducted immediately after shadowing the CS's sales calls. We deconstructed their CS's perception of how they felt about the meeting. Combined with the shadowing, the RI will focus on getting the CS to explain from their point of view what went on procedurally. We will focus on drawing out their internal thought processes and feelings during and after the sales calls.

Critical Incident Technique (CIT)

The Critical Incidents Technique is a method for collecting behavioral observations that have critical significance to accomplish specific goal. Critical incidents data may be gathered by asking CS about the experience they had. For example, CS will be asked about their experience with certain prescriber. The main accents of the interview will concern the following: detailed description of how the meeting went, what were CS's emotions and thoughts, were any conclusions made about changing the CS behavior in the future?

People Mapping

People mapping is a participatory activity where we ask participants to visually describe where they fit into their organization. Participants are asked to map out the different roles and titles in their organization, business relationships, and types of information exchanged. We guided them through a drawing activity where they will plot out key stakeholders and drew the communication channels between them.

I Like, I Wish

A structured way for people to express their attitudes about a current product or service they use. The statement format asks them to say what works well about the status quo and what areas of improvement could be.

Data Analysis and Synthesis

To analyze the data gathered during research and understand the big picture of our usersâ€™ ecosystem we used affinity diagramming technique. It is a bottom-up approach used to organize key ideas from different users in one place. We also synthesized data by building flow and cultural models. A flow model demonstrates the connections between people and flow of ideas, requests and artifacts. A cultural model is a diagram that helps understand the influences of other parties on a participant that eventually impact his/her work. Finally, we combined all our flow models into one big consolidated flow model. Based on our analysis and synthesis, we identified areas for potential improvement that we used for designing and prototyping a solution over the Summer.
